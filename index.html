What’s waiting inside the gates

Built for a full day of fun

  • Rides & midway

    A full midway of rides and games for every age and every level of bravery, from kiddie coasters to the big spinners.

  • Fair food

    Funnel cakes, corn dogs, cotton candy, and the once-a-year favorites you plan the whole visit around.

  • Free shows & music

    Every show is free with gate admission — headline acts, strolling performers, and the Parkway Stage.

  • Competitions & exhibits

    Livestock, crafts, baking, and live demonstrations — the classic fair traditions, judged and on display all week.

Why this fair exists

Every ticket gives back

The fair is owned and run by the Exchange Club of Charleston, a nonprofit. When the lights go down, the proceeds go out — to local charities, schools, and students across the Lowcountry.

$504K+granted to 90+ local nonprofits and students after the latest fair

15,000+volunteer hours from Exchange Club members in a single season

$11M+returned to the Lowcountry community since 2003

Ways to save

Deal days worth planning around

How deal days ran in 2025 — the 2026 lineup is announced closer to opening.

  • Bring-a-friend Thursdays

    Buy one Circle K admission ticket and a friend walks in free at the gate.

  • Heroes Fridays

    Free admission plus one guest for military, veterans, and first responders with valid ID.

  • 2-fer Tuesday

    Buy one ride wristband at the gate or midway booth, get a second one free.

  • Senior Day

    $5 admission for guests 55+, one free caregiver admission each, with morning programming.

Since 1957

A Lowcountry tradition, seven decades strong

From Charleston fairgrounds in the 1920s to 180+ acres at Exchange Park in Ladson, the fair has moved, grown, and survived a fire — and never lost the plot: raise money, give it away, have a great time doing it.
