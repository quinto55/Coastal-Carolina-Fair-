Exchange Park · Ladson, SC

Home of the fair since 1979

More than 180 acres on Highway 78, with parking for 10,000+ cars — and it works year-round, not just eleven days a fall.

The grounds

  • Agriculture Building

    7,000 square feet with a farmers-market feel during the fair — livestock, growers, and local producers.

  • Lakefront Stage

    Every headliner plays here, beside the park’s one-acre lake — free with admission, first-come seating.

  • Exchange Hall

    The park’s newest indoor venue — atrium entry, modern finishes, and flexible space for events year-round.

  • Freedom Hall

    22,000+ square feet of exhibit space with a commercial kitchen, food booth, heat and A/C — the fair’s commercial exhibit home.

The full park, by the numbers

  • 180+ landscaped acres with a 1-acre lake
  • 10,000+ free parking spaces
  • Paved midway
  • Parkway Stage — 1,500 sq ft, dressing rooms, seating for 500
  • Gazebo Stage — 1,400 sq ft, bleachers for 300
  • 40,000 sq ft horse show ring with bleachers
  • 7,000 sq ft Agriculture Building
  • 175+ RV spaces with water, sewer, and electric

Weddings, trade shows, RV rallies, equestrian events, concerts — the park rents year-round. Call 843.572.3161 to talk dates.

A century of showing up

How we got here

  1. 1922 Charleston County’s first fairs open at College Park, later moving among city sites including Marion Square.
  2. 1930s–50s Fairs run on the Johnson Hagood Stadium grounds through the war years and after.
  3. 1957 The Exchange Club of Charleston, hunting for a way to raise money for charity, founds the modern Coastal Carolina Fair.
  4. 1958 Fire destroys the original exhibit building — the fair carries on from the stadium bleachers.
  5. 1960s–70s Two decades on Dorchester Road as the fair outgrows every home it has.
  6. 1979 I-526 construction pushes the fair to Ladson — 180+ acres that finally fit it. Exchange Park is born.
  7. Today Eleven days each fall, run by volunteers, with proceeds granted back to the Lowcountry — $11 million and counting since 2003.
