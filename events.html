What fills eleven days

2026 lineup coming late summer. Until then, filter by what you’re into — this is what a fair week always holds.

  • Lakefront Stage headliners

    A different act every night of the run, free with admission. Seating is first-come.

  • Parkway Stage

    Local bands, dance groups, and school ensembles daily — 1,500 square feet of stage with seating for 500. Apply to perform.

  • Strolling performers

    Roaming acts work the midway all day — you don’t find them, they find you.

  • Creative Arts

    Ceramics, quilts, needle crafts, sewing, baked and canned goods, woodworking, decorative painting — the exhibit halls fill with ribbons on the line.

  • Fine Art & Youth Art Shows

    Adult fine art alongside the Tricounty's best student work.

  • Fiddle Contest

    The annual Coastal Carolina Fair Fiddling Contest — six years running.

  • King Arthur Baking Contest

    Bake your best with King Arthur Flour; pre-registration closes just before opening week.

  • Honey Competition

    Lowcountry beekeepers go jar to jar.

  • Chili Cookoff

    Competition spots fill fast — come watch the judging even if you missed the entry window.

  • 4-H Programs

    The backbone of local fairs across the country, judged and displayed all run.

  • Arena Competitions

    Bull riding, animal exhibitions, and more in the show ring.

  • Live demonstrations

    Craftspeople and makers at work daily in the exhibit areas — watch, ask, learn.

  • Senior Day

    A mid-run weekday tradition: $5 admission for guests 55+, free caregiver admission, and dedicated morning programming.

  • Heroes Days

    Free admission honoring military members, veterans, and first responders — details on the deals page.
