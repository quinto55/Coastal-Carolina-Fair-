FAQ · 2026

Ask us anything

The questions every fairgoer has, answered straight. Can’t find yours? Call 843.572.3161.

Tickets

Where should I buy tickets?

Only two places: the fair’s official website, or participating Circle K stores. Online tickets scan straight from your phone at the gate.

Someone on Facebook is selling fair tickets. Should I buy them?

No. Tickets from social media sellers and resellers can’t be verified — and often aren’t real. If it didn’t come from the official site or a Circle K, the gate can’t honor it.

Are concerts and shows extra?

Never. Every show on the grounds — headline acts included — is free with your gate admission. Rides and games are separate.

What if it rains?

The fair runs rain or shine, and tickets aren’t refunded or rain-checked. With eleven days in the run, pick a day you’re confident about.

At the gate

What are the gate hours?

The usual pattern: weekday gates open at 3 PM, Saturdays run 10 AM – 10 PM, Sundays noon – 9 PM. Confirmed 2026 hours are published with the full schedule — see Plan Your Visit.

What does parking cost?

General parking is free, and Exchange Park holds more than 10,000 cars. A paid VIP option parks you steps from the gate.

What’s the bag policy?

Clear bags only. Medically necessary items are the exception and may be inspected at the gate.

What can’t I bring?

Weapons, alcohol, backpacks, glass, and coolers stay home. Costumes are welcome around Halloween, but skip full-face masks, capes, and toy weapons.

Can I bring my dog?

Only ADA service animals — trained dogs and miniature horses — are allowed on the grounds. Emotional support animals and pets can’t come in.

Inside the fair

Is the fair wheelchair accessible?

Yes — and wheelchair and mobility device rentals are available on the grounds, first-come, first-served. Arrive early on busy days.

Can I smoke or vape?

Smoking and vaping are restricted to designated areas — look for signage or ask any staff member.

Is there a day for seniors?

Yes — Senior Day is a fair tradition: $5 admission for guests 55 and up, one free caregiver admission each, and dedicated morning programming. The 2026 date is announced with the schedule.

Where does the money go?

To the Lowcountry. The Exchange Club of Charleston runs the fair as a nonprofit and grants the proceeds to local charities, schools, and students — more than $11 million since 2003. Read the story.
