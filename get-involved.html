Get Involved

The fair runs on people like you

Fifteen thousand volunteer hours go into every season. Vend, volunteer, perform, compete — or put the fair’s proceeds to work in your community.

Five ways in

  • Become a vendor

    Three ways in: Freedom Hall commercial booths (225,000+ average attendance walking past), Agriculture Building farmers-market spots (≈10×10 ft), and outdoor food-trailer concessions. Outdoor units must pass Health Department inspection; all vendors carry $1M general liability (purchasable through the fair). Returning vendors get first refusal, so apply early.

    Apply on the official site →

  • Volunteer

    Exchange Club members and community volunteers staff the gates, exhibits, and grounds — it’s the engine of the whole operation.

    Volunteer info →

  • Perform on the Parkway Stage

    School groups, bands, dancers, and local acts play daily slots throughout the fair. If you’ve got a show, there’s a stage.

    Performer application →

  • Enter a competition

    Livestock, baking, canning, quilts, art, photography — bring your best and take home a ribbon. Categories and entry windows post before the fair.

    Competition details →

  • Apply for a grant

    The Exchange Club of Charleston Foundation grants fair proceeds to Lowcountry nonprofits and students — $504,212 went to more than 90 recipients after a recent season.

    Grant information →
